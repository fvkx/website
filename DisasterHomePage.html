Earthquake

Description: Sudden shaking of the ground caused by movement of Earth's crust, which can damage buildings and infrastructure.

How to Avoid:

  • Secure heavy furniture and shelves to walls.
  • Prepare an emergency kit with essentials.
  • Know safe spots in every room (under a sturdy table or corner).

What to Do If It Happens:

  • Drop, Cover, and Hold On.
  • Stay indoors until the shaking stops.
  • If outside, move away from buildings and power lines.

Typhoon

Description: A powerful tropical storm with strong winds and heavy rain that can cause flooding and destruction.

How to Avoid:

  • Always check weather advisories.
  • Trim trees and secure loose objects outside.
  • Store emergency food, water, and batteries.

What to Do If It Happens:

  • Stay indoors and keep away from windows.
  • Turn off electrical power if flooding occurs.
  • Evacuate to higher ground when advised by authorities.

Flood

Description: Overflow of water that submerges land, often caused by heavy rain or storm surges.

How to Avoid:

  • Avoid dumping garbage that blocks drainage systems.
  • Build homes above known flood levels.
  • Always monitor rainfall and water levels.

What to Do If It Happens:

  • Move to higher ground immediately.
  • Turn off gas and electricity.
  • Do not walk or drive through floodwaters.

Fire

Description: Uncontrolled burning that can spread quickly and cause severe damage to property and life.

How to Avoid:

  • Keep flammable materials away from heat sources.
  • Install smoke alarms in every room.
  • Never leave cooking unattended.

What to Do If It Happens:

  • Stay low and crawl under smoke.
  • Use a wet cloth to cover your nose and mouth.
  • Evacuate immediately and call emergency services.

Landslide

Description: Movement of rock, soil, or debris down a slope due to rain, earthquakes, or human activity.

How to Avoid:

  • Avoid building on steep slopes.
  • Plant trees and vegetation to prevent erosion.
  • Monitor cracks in walls or ground after heavy rains.

What to Do If It Happens:

  • Move away from the path of the slide immediately.
  • Go to higher, stable ground.
  • Listen to local authorities for evacuation updates.
